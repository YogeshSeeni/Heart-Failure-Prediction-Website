Heart Failure

Cardiovascular diseases are a leading cause of death worldwide, with an estimated 17.9 million deaths annually, accounting for 31% of all global deaths. A significant majority of these deaths, 80%, are caused by heart attacks and strokes. Furthermore, a significant portion of these deaths, one-third, occur in individuals under the age of 70.

Heart failure is a prevalent outcome of cardiovascular diseases, and this study utilizes a dataset featuring 11 distinct characteristics to predict the likelihood of heart disease.

Research

The goal of this project is to maximize the accuracy of a deep neural network in predicting heart failure by changing epochs. A neural network is a type of machine learning algorithm that is able to recognize patterns and make predictions based on input data. An epoch is one iteration through a dataset used for training. During an epoch, the neural network is presented with input data it uses to make a prediction based on its current parameters. These predictions are compared to the true output, and the network attempts to optimize the parameters to reduce the error in its prediction.
